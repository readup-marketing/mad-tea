Alice Leaves the Tea Party

October 24th, 2025

"Clean cup! Move along!" the Hatter and March Hare suddenly shouted in unison. They all shifted one place around the table.

Alice was bewildered by this abrupt command, but she moved to a new seat nonetheless. The Dormouse stirred and occupied the seat where Alice had been sitting.

"Now we can have some amusement!" thought Alice. "Perhaps they'll ask me a riddle I can actually solve."

But instead, the March Hare addressed the Dormouse, "Tell us a story!"

"Yes, please do!" pleaded Alice.

"And be swift about it," added the Hatter, "or you'll be asleep again before you finish."

"Once upon a time there were three little sisters," the Dormouse began hurriedly, "and their names were Elsie, Lacie, and Tillie. They lived at the bottom of a well‚Äî"

"What did they live on?" said Alice, who always took great interest in questions of eating and drinking.

"They lived on treacle," said the Dormouse, after contemplating for a moment.

"They couldn't have done that," Alice said gently. "They'd have been ill."

"So they were," said the Dormouse, "very ill."

Alice tried to imagine what such an extraordinary way of living would be like, but it perplexed her too much. So she asked, "But why did they live at the bottom of a well?"

"Have some more tea," the March Hare said to Alice very earnestly.

Alice had endured quite enough of the mad tea party. She rose to her feet with considerable dignity and walked away. The Dormouse fell asleep instantly, and the other two took no notice whatsoever of her departure, though she glanced back once or twice hoping they might call after her.

"That's the most ridiculous tea party I ever attended in my entire life!" she declared to herself as she walked through the wood.

But even as she left, Alice couldn't help smiling at the memory of their peculiar logic and endless word games. Wonderland might be bewildering, but it certainly was never dull.

The End
