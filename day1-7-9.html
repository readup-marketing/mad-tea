The Mad Tea Party

October 20th, 2024

‚è±Ô∏è 5 min read + activities

Alice had been walking through Wonderland when she saw a large table set out under a tree. The March Hare and the Mad Hatter were sitting at it having tea, with a Dormouse fast asleep between them. They were using the sleeping Dormouse as a cushion to rest their arms on while they talked.

"That looks uncomfortable for the Dormouse," thought Alice, "but since it's asleep, I suppose it doesn't mind."

The table was enormous, but the three of them were crowded together at one corner. "No room! No room!" they shouted when they saw Alice coming.

"There's plenty of room!" said Alice, looking at all the empty chairs. She sat down in a large armchair without waiting to be asked.

"Have some wine," the March Hare said in a friendly way.

Alice looked around the table, but there was nothing on it except tea. "I don't see any wine," she said.

"There isn't any," said the March Hare.

"Then it wasn't very polite of you to offer it," said Alice, feeling cross.

The Hatter stared at her with very wide eyes. "Why is a raven like a writing-desk?" he asked.

Alice felt excited. Maybe she could guess the answer! "I think I can work that out," she said.

"Do you mean you think you can find out the answer?" said the March Hare.

"Yes," said Alice.

"Then you should say what you mean," said the March Hare.

But would Alice ever solve this mysterious riddle, or would the tea party's strange logic confuse her?
