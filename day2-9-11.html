"Have you guessed the riddle yet?" the Hatter asked, turning to Alice again.

"No, I give up," Alice replied. "What's the answer?"

"I haven't got the slightest idea," said the Hatter happily.

"Neither have I," said the March Hare.

Alice sighed. "Don't you think you could do something better with your time than ask riddles that have no answers?"

"If you knew Time as well as I do," said the Hatter, "you wouldn't talk about wasting it. Time is sentient, not just a thing."

"I don't understand what you mean," said Alice.

"Of course you don't!" the Hatter said, shaking his head dismissively. "I'd wager you've never even conversed with Time!"

"Perhaps not," Alice said cautiously, "but I know I must keep time when I study music."

Today's Activities

Complete these inference activities with a parent or grown-up

üîç Character Inference

Question 1:

Why does the Hatter speak to Alice dismissively when she says she doesn't understand?

Think about: How does the Hatter see himself compared to Alice? What does his tone tell us about his character?

Question 2:

The Hatter says Time "refuses to cooperate" with him. What does this suggest about their relationship?

Consider: What might have happened between them? Why would Time stop helping the Hatter?

Question 3:

Why do you think the Hatter uses the pronoun "him" when talking about Time instead of "it"?

Think about: How does personifying Time change how we understand the Hatter's relationship with it?

Question 4:

What can we infer about Alice's character when she says "perhaps not" cautiously?

Consider: Why is Alice being careful with her words? What has she learned about Wonderland?
