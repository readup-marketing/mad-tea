The Mad Tea Party

October 23rd, 2025

‚è±Ô∏è 5 min read + activities

"What a strange watch!" Alice said, looking at the Hatter's pocket watch. "It tells you what day of the month it is, but it doesn't tell you what time it is!"

"Why should it?" muttered the Hatter. "Does your watch tell you what year it is?"

"Of course not," Alice said quickly, "but that's because it stays the same year for such a long time."

"Which is just the same with mine," said the Hatter.

Alice felt very puzzled. The Hatter's words didn't seem to make any sense, even though they were speaking English.

"The Dormouse is asleep again," said the Hatter, and he poured a little hot tea on its nose.

The Dormouse shook its head crossly but didn't open its eyes. "Of course, of course; that's just what I was going to say myself."

"Have you guessed the riddle yet?" the Hatter said, turning to Alice again.

"No, I give up," Alice replied. "What's the answer?"

"I haven't got the slightest idea," said the Hatter.

"Neither have I," said the March Hare.

Alice sighed. This wasn't getting anywhere.

"I think you should do something better with your time," said Alice, "than ask riddles with no answers."

"Have some more tea," the March Hare said to Alice very seriously.

"I haven't had any tea yet," Alice replied, sounding annoyed, "so I can't have more."

"You mean you can't have less," said the Hatter. "It's very easy to have more than nothing."

"Nobody asked for your opinion," said Alice.

"Who's making rude comments now?" the Hatter asked with a triumphant smile.

Alice was getting very frustrated with their word games. But something strange was about to happen.
