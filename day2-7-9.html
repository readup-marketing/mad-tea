"Have you guessed the riddle yet?" the Hatter asked, turning to Alice again.

"No, I give up," Alice replied. "What's the answer?"

"I haven't got the slightest idea," said the Hatter cheerfully.

"Neither have I," said the March Hare.

Alice sighed tiredly. "I think you should do something better with your time than ask riddles that have no answers."

"If you knew Time as well as I do," said the Hatter, "you wouldn't talk about wasting it. Time is a person, not a thing."

"I don't understand what you mean," said Alice.

"Of course you don't!" the Hatter said, shaking his head in a rude way. "I bet you've never even talked to Time!"

"Maybe not," Alice said carefully, "but I know I have to beat time when I learn music."

"Ah! That explains it," said the Hatter. "Time won't let me do that anymore. Now, if you were friends with him, he'd do almost anything you wanted.
