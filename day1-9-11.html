The Mad Tea Party

October 20th, 2024

‚è±Ô∏è 6 min read + activities

Alice had been wandering through Wonderland when she discovered a large table positioned beneath an ancient oak tree. The March Hare and the Mad Hatter were occupied with drinking tea, with a Dormouse in a deep slumber between them. They were using the Dormouse as a cushion to rest their elbows on whilst they engaged in conversation.

"That looks rather uncomfortable for the Dormouse," thought Alice, "but since it's unconscious, I don't believe it minds."

The table was vast, but the three of them were clustered together at one corner. "No room! No room!" they exclaimed loudly when they saw Alice approaching.

"There's plenty of room!" said Alice, observing all the vacant space. She sat down in a large armchair without being invited.

"Have some wine," the March Hare offered graciously.

Alice examined the table, but there was nothing upon it except tea. "I don't see any wine," she remarked.

"There isn't any," said the March Hare.

"Then it wasn't very civil of you to offer it," said Alice, feeling indignant.

The Hatter fixed his gaze upon her with extraordinarily dilated eyes. "Why is a raven like a writing-desk?" he enquired mysteriously.

Alice experienced a surge of enthusiasm. Perhaps she could decipher the answer! "I believe I can solve that riddle," she declared.

"Do you mean to suggest you think you can discover the answer?" said the March Hare.

"Precisely," said Alice.

"Then you should articulate what you intend," said the March Hare.

But would Alice ever decipher this enigmatic riddle, or would the tea party's absurd logic perplex her?
